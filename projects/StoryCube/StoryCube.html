StoryCube: Tangible Prompt Engineering for Pre-Literate Children

Maximilian Egger, Pascal Knierim,
University of Innsburck
CHI EA 2026
Paper CHI EA 2026

StoryCube enables children to prompt generative AI to create short stories using tangible tokens that magnetically attach to the cube. From left: A preschooler places tokens on StoryCube during use. The StoryCube device with attached tokens. The collection of tangible story tokens used to compose prompts.

Abstract

Preschool children are largely excluded from interacting with LLMs because current prompt interfaces rely on fluent speech, writing, or reading. As generative AI increasingly pervades children's everyday environments, they need age-appropriate, accessible ways to express intent and gain agency in AI-mediated experiences. We introduce StoryCube, a tangible prompt engineering interface that lets children compose structured prompts using physical story tokens to generate audio stories. StoryCube is novel in enabling structured prompting through tangible interaction, supporting pre-literacy access and collaborative, hands-on prompt composition rather than text- or voice-based prompting. In a preliminary study with six children (ages 5–8) and two educators, children used StoryCube intuitively with minimal guidance, explored diverse token combinations, and reported high enjoyment, while also noting mixed story quality. Findings suggest that tangible prompt engineering is a promising concept for child-centered AI interaction, motivating further research on parametrization, token grammar, safety, and educational integration.
